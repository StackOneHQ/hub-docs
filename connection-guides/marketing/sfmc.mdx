<Warning>
  Ensure you have `Installed Package | Administer` permission. This permission is automatically applied to the Administrator and Marketing Cloud Administrator system-defined roles. Add the permission for a different role or user in the Administration area.
</Warning>

If you've been directed to StackOne to integrate with Salesforce Marketing Cloud, the following steps will help you understand the process and any necessary actions to configure successful integration.

## Create a package (server to server integration)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Setup">
    Hover over profile icon and click on the setup option.
    <Frame>
      <img className='rounded-md' style={{ margin: '0 auto', border: '1px solid #efefef' }} alt="Open Setup" src="/images/sfmc/image.png" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Navigate to Installed Packages">
    Click Apps and then click Installed Packages.
    <Frame>
      <img className='rounded-md' style={{ margin: '0 auto', border: '1px solid #efefef' }} alt="Navigate to Installed Packages" src="/images/sfmc/image2.png" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create new Package">
    Click on `new` button placed on top-right corner on the installed packages page. 
    <Frame>
      <img className='rounded-md' style={{ margin: '0 auto', border: '1px solid #efefef' }} alt="Create new Package" src="/images/sfmc/image3.png" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Fill the details">
    Fill the name of package and description and click on `save` button.
    <Frame>
        <img className='rounded-md' style={{ margin: '0 auto', border: '1px solid #efefef' }} alt="Fill the details" src="/images/sfmc/image4.png" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Component">
    Now it's time to add components, click on the `Add Component` button.
    <Frame>
        <img className='rounded-md' style={{ margin: '0 auto', border: '1px solid #efefef' }} alt="Add Component" src="/images/sfmc/image5.png" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose your component type">
    Select `API Integration` and click on `next` button.
    <Frame>
        <img className='rounded-md' style={{ margin: '0 auto', border: '1px solid #efefef' }} alt="Choose your component type" src="/images/sfmc/image6.png" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose your integration type">
    Select `Server to Server` and click on `next` button.
    <Frame>
        <img className='rounded-md' style={{ margin: '0 auto', border: '1px solid #efefef' }} alt="Choose your integration type" src="/images/sfmc/image7.png" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set Server-to-Server Properties">
    Check all the Assets scope and click on save.
    <Frame>
        <img className='rounded-md' style={{ margin: '0 auto', border: '1px solid #efefef' }} alt="Set Server-to-Server Properties" src="/images/sfmc/image8.png" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Get the credentials">
    Now the Credentials are visible on screen, use them to integrate with StackOne.
    <Frame>
        <img className='rounded-md' style={{ margin: '0 auto', border: '1px solid #efefef' }} alt="Set Server-to-Server Properties" src="/images/sfmc/image9.png" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Connecting with StackOne

<Steps>
<Step title="Enter your API credentials">
    Upon reaching the Link Account page, enter the following credentials received in the previous steps:
    - Client ID
    - Client Secret
    - Authentication Base URI

    Proceed by clicking the `Connect` button.

    <Frame>
      <img className='rounded-md' style={{ margin: '0 auto', border: '1px solid #efefef' }} alt="Enter your API credentials" src="/images/sfmc/image10.png" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>


## Linking your Account

Enter the required fields from the previous steps in your connection form.
