  Twilio's underlying Organizations API is currently in private beta. To enable this feature, reach out to your Twilio account executive or [Twilio Support](https://help.twilio.com/articles/360048500694#h_01HTDZ3N3VFM4QXHRZR5MSFS6A).

<Warning>
  Ensure that your Twilio account has Organization Owner or Organization Admin privileges.
</Warning>

If you've been directed to StackOne to integrate with Twilio, the following steps will help you understand the process and any necessary actions to configure successful integration.

## Log in to the Twilio Admin Dashboard

<Steps>
    <Step title="Log In">
      Log in to your Organization Owner or Organization Admin account on [Twilio](https://www.twilio.com/).
    </Step>

    <Step title="Admin Dashboard">
      This integration supports one Twilio Organization per linked account.

      Click the **Admin** drop-down in the top right, then click [**Organization > Twilio Admin**](https://admin.twilio.com/) for your Organization.
      <Frame>
      <img className='rounded-md' style={{ margin: '0 auto', border: '1px solid #efefef' }} alt="Link Account" src="/images/twilio/image1.png" />
    </Frame>
    </Step>
</Steps>

## Get your Twilio Organization SID

<Steps>
    <Step title="Organization SID">
      In your Twilio Admin Dashboard, your Organization SID will be displayed on the main **Overview** page.

      Store this value safely to be used in the next steps.
      <Frame>
      <img className='rounded-md' style={{ margin: '0 auto', border: '1px solid #efefef' }} alt="Organization SID" src="/images/twilio/image7.png" />
    </Frame>
    </Step>
</Steps>

## Create a New OAuth App
<Steps>
  <Step title="View OAuth Apps">
      In the Admin Dashboard, click **Applications > OAuth Apps** on the left.
      <Frame>
      <img className='rounded-md' style={{ margin: '0 auto', border: '1px solid #efefef' }} alt="View OAuth Apps" src="/images/twilio/image2.png" />
    </Frame>
    </Step>

    <Step title="Create a new OAuth App">
      Click the **+ Create OAuth application** button on the right.
      <Frame>
      <img className='rounded-md' style={{ margin: '0 auto', border: '1px solid #efefef' }} alt="Create a new OAuth App" src="/images/twilio/image3.png" />
    </Frame>
    </Step>

    <Step title="Select App Grant Type">
      Under Grant Type, select the **Client credential** option.
      <Frame>
      <img className='rounded-md' style={{ margin: '0 auto', border: '1px solid #efefef' }} alt="Select App Grant Type" src="/images/twilio/image4.png" />
    </Frame>
    </Step>

    <Step title="Enter App Details">
      Enter a name for this new App under Application name.

      Select the following *Scopes & permissions*:
      <ul>
        <li>role-assignments: List</li>
        <li>managed-accounts: Read, List</li>
        <li>managed-users: Read, List</li>
      </ul>

      When complete, click the **Save** button.
      <Frame>
      <img className='rounded-md' style={{ margin: '0 auto', border: '1px solid #efefef' }} alt="Select App Grant Type" src="/images/twilio/image5.png" />
    </Frame>

    </Step>

    <Step title="Copy App Credentials">
      Your new **Client ID** and **Client Secret** will be displayed. **Copy** and store these values safely to use in the next steps.

      When done, select the *"Got it! ...* checkbox. Then click the **Done** button.
      <Frame>
      <img className='rounded-md' style={{ margin: '0 auto', border: '1px solid #efefef' }} alt="Copy App Credentials" src="/images/twilio/image6.png" />
    </Frame>
    </Step>
</Steps>

## Connecting with StackOne

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter Credentials">
    Upon reaching the Link Account page, enter the credentials from the previous steps:
    - Client ID
    - Client Secret
    - Organization SID

    Proceed by clicking the Connect button.

  </Step>
</Steps>
